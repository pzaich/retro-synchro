# Artistic Swimming Elements - Comprehensive Reference for Video Game

## Research Summary

Based on World Aquatics (formerly FINA) official rules, the English Artistic Swimming grading system, Inside Synchro resources, and competition documentation, here is a comprehensive catalogue of 88 distinct elements organized by category.

---

## 1. FIGURES / BODY POSITIONS (18 elements)

These are the foundational body positions that form the basis of all movements.

| # | Element Name | Description | Difficulty |
|---|-------------|-------------|------------|
| 1 | **Back Layout** | Body floats straight, face-up on surface, sculling under hips | Beginner |
| 2 | **Front Layout** | Body floats straight, face-down on surface, sculling by chest | Beginner |
| 3 | **Tub** | Both legs pulled to chest, shins and tops of feet dry and parallel on surface | Beginner |
| 4 | **Ballet Leg** | Back layout with one leg extended perpendicular to body, other parallel to surface | Beginner |
| 5 | **Double Ballet Leg** | Both legs extended and held perpendicular to the body | Intermediate |
| 6 | **Flamingo** | Like ballet leg but bottom leg is pulled into chest, shin touching knee of vertical leg | Intermediate |
| 7 | **Bent Knee (Heron)** | Vertical body position, one leg vertical, other bends with toe touching knee of vertical leg | Intermediate |
| 8 | **Crane** | Vertical body, one leg vertical, other dropped parallel to surface making 90-degree "L" shape | Intermediate |
| 9 | **Fishtail** | Like crane but requires the bottom foot to be at the surface | Intermediate |
| 10 | **Side Fishtail** | One leg vertical, other extended to the side parallel to water, creating a "Y" shape | Intermediate |
| 11 | **Knight** | Surface arch position with legs flat on surface, body arched, one leg lifted to vertical | Advanced |
| 12 | **Split Position** | Body vertical, one leg stretched forward along surface, other extended back (upside-down splits) | Advanced |
| 13 | **Vertical** | Body completely straight, upside-down, perpendicular to surface, both legs out of water | Intermediate |
| 14 | **Surface Arch** | Body arched at the surface, head vertically in line with hips, legs flat on surface | Intermediate |
| 15 | **Back Pike** | Body bent at hips in pike position while face-up, legs straight | Intermediate |
| 16 | **Front Pike** | Body bent at hips in pike position while face-down, legs straight | Intermediate |
| 17 | **Submerged Back Pike** | Back pike position performed fully submerged | Advanced |
| 18 | **Inverted Tuck** | Body curled in tuck position while inverted underwater | Beginner |

## 3. SCULLING / ARM MOVEMENTS (12 elements)

Sculls are hand movements used to propel and stabilize the body. They are the engine of all artistic swimming.

| # | Element Name | Description | Difficulty |
|---|-------------|-------------|------------|
| 39 | **Support Scull** | Upper arms against sides, forearms at 90 degrees, moved back and forth. Used to support body upside-down | Beginner |
| 40 | **Stationary Scull** | Maintains position without forward/backward movement | Beginner |
| 41 | **Propeller Scull** | Propels the body headfirst across the surface | Beginner |
| 42 | **Torpedo Scull** | Propels body feet-first in a streamlined position | Intermediate |
| 43 | **Alligator Scull** | Propels body headfirst while in a back layout with arms at sides | Intermediate |
| 44 | **Paddle Scull** | Used for lateral or directional movement | Intermediate |
| 45 | **Canoe Scull** | One-armed scull used for turning and curved travel | Intermediate |
| 46 | **Barrel Scull** | Creates rotation of the body along its long axis | Advanced |
| 47 | **Split Scull** | Used to support body during split position elements | Advanced |
| 48 | **Spinning Scull** | Generates rapid rotation while in vertical position | Advanced |
| 49 | **Totem Scull** | Specialized scull for totem-style vertical support | Advanced |
| 50 | **Eggbeater Kick** | Continuous alternating circular breaststroke kick for treading/lifting. The fundamental power source | Beginner |

| 55 | **Balance Lift** | B - Balance | Featured swimmer sits/stands/lays on base swimmer(s) and is lifted up and away from water surface | Advanced |

| 57 | **Platform with Dismount** | P - Platform | Platform lift ending with a jump or dismount | Elite |
| 58 | **Combined Airborne-Balance** | C - Combined | Combines airborne and balance characteristics in sequence | Elite |

| # | Element Name | Description | Difficulty |
|---|-------------|-------------|------------|
| 63 | **Spin 180** | Half rotation (180 degrees) while in vertical position | Beginner |
| 64 | **Spin Up 360** | Full ascending spin (360 degrees), rising while rotating | Intermediate |
| 65 | **Spin Down 360** | Full descending spin while sinking in vertical | Intermediate |
| 66 | **Combined Spin 360+360** | Ascending spin followed by descending spin (or vice versa), 720 total | Advanced |
| 67 | **Continuous Spin 720** | Rapid descending spin of at least 720 degrees before heels reach surface | Elite |
| 68 | **Continuous Spin 1080** | Three full rapid descending rotations | Elite |
| 69 | **Twist 180** | 180-degree rotation at a constant height (no ascending/descending) | Intermediate |
| 70 | **Twist 360** | Full rotation at constant height | Advanced |

## 6. FORMATIONS / PATTERNS (10 elements)

Patterns are the geometric formations that teams hold during choreography. These are scored as part of artistic impression and synchronization.

| # | Element Name | Description | Difficulty |
|---|-------------|-------------|------------|
| 71 | **Circle** | All swimmers arranged in a ring, often rotating | Beginner |
| 72 | **Straight Line** | All swimmers in a single row | Beginner |
| 73 | **Diagonal Line** | Swimmers in an angled line across the pool | Beginner |
| 74 | **V-Formation** | Swimmers in a "V" or chevron shape | Intermediate |
| 75 | **Diamond** | Four or more swimmers forming a diamond/rhombus | Intermediate |
| 76 | **Square / Block** | Swimmers forming a rectangle or square grid | Intermediate |
| 77 | **Triangle** | Swimmers in a triangular arrangement | Intermediate |
| 78 | **Star / Pinwheel** | Swimmers radiating from a center point, often with elbows/wrists joined in multi-armed waves | Advanced |
| 79 | **Staggered / Offset Lines** | Parallel lines with offset positions for visual depth | Advanced |
| 80 | **Pattern Change (Transition)** | Morphing from one formation to another during choreography. Each pattern change is worth 0.3 bonus | Advanced |

## 7. HYBRID ELEMENTS (8 elements)

Hybrids are combinations of 5+ leg movements performed with the head underneath the hips. They are unique to each routine, built from a catalogue of skills across six movement families.

| # | Element Name / Family | Description | Difficulty |
|---|----------------------|-------------|------------|
| 81 | **Thrust Family** | Rapid upward movements from submerged pike to vertical (Levels B through 10) | Varies |
| 82 | **Spin Family** | Ascending, descending, or combined spins in vertical (Levels B through 10) | Varies |
| 83 | **Twist Family** | Rotations at the same height in vertical (Levels B through 10) | Varies |
| 84 | **Walkover / Transition Family** | Arcing movements through split, crane, knight positions | Varies |
| 85 | **Position Change Family** | Transitions between named body positions underwater | Varies |
| 86 | **Vertical Descent** | Controlled sinking in vertical position at the same tempo as the preceding movement | Intermediate |
| 87 | **Custom Hybrid Sequence** | Coach-designed combination of 5+ skills from the catalogue with declared difficulty | Varies |
| 88 | **Head First Boost and Descent** | Start from underwater, eggbeater pop upward, arms at sides, then controlled descent | Advanced |

## SCORING AND DIFFICULTY SYSTEM OVERVIEW

### World Aquatics Difficulty Structure:
- **Base Mark (BM):** Every free hybrid has a BM of 0.5; pair acrobatics have BM of 0.1
- **Declared Difficulty (DD):** Sum of individual skill difficulty values + base value
- **Six Movement Families:** Each has difficulty levels from Basic (B) up to Level 10
- **Element Execution:** Scored 0-10 by five judges (drop high/low, average remaining 3), multiplied by DD
- **Pattern Change Bonus:** Each formation transition = 0.3 bonus

### Competition Structure:
- **Technical Routines:** Include Technical Required Elements (TREs) + free hybrids + acrobatics
- **Free Routines:** Only hybrids and acrobatics (more creative freedom)
- **Acrobatic Routines:** 7 acrobatics required, at least one from each group (A, B, P, C)
- **Figures Competitions:** For younger athletes (under 16), performing individual figures without music

### English Grading System (game progression reference):
- **Grade 1:** Basic positions and layouts (back layout, front pike, tub, hanging vertical)
- **Grade 2:** Ballet leg, somersaults, blossom, neptunus, walkout front
- **Grade 3:** More complex transitions and combined movements
- **Grade 4:** Flying fish 360, cyclone, barracuda airborne split, swordfish, whip 720, saturn, albatross 360
- **Grade 5:** Most demanding international-level figures

## GAME DESIGN NOTES

1. **Progression curve:** The English grading system (Grades 1-5) maps naturally to game difficulty tiers
2. **Composability:** Hybrids are built from combining individual skills -- this maps well to a combo/chaining system
3. **Declared difficulty mechanic:** The real sport's "declare your DD then execute" mechanic translates to a risk/reward system
4. **Scoring multiplier:** Execution score x Difficulty = final score (natural game scoring formula)
5. **Team vs Solo:** Solo/duet/team events offer different gameplay modes
6. **Acrobatic types:** The four acrobatic groups (A/B/P/C) provide natural ability trees or unlock categories
7. **Pattern bonuses:** Formation changes during team routines could be a team-coordination mechanic
